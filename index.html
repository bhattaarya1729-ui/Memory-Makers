The studio

Memory Makers started with one 35mm camera, a spare bathroom turned darkroom, and the stubborn belief that a photograph should feel like a memory, not a performance.

Today we're a small team of photographers and one very opinionated cat who supervises every shoot from a windowsill. We still shoot on film for a portion of every session, because grain and light leaks have never once lied to us.

We work out of natural light studios and, more often, wherever your story actually happens: a kitchen table, a wedding aisle, a hospital hallway, a rooftop at golden hour.
